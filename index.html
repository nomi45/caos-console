C

Clarify

Name the real decision and the real deadline

Most stress doesn't come from hard decisions — it comes from unclear ones. This page turns a vague worry into one clear question you can actually answer.

Example

Sarah is deciding when to launch her Gumroad product

Decision I'm avoiding: "I keep going back and forth on whether to launch my Gumroad product now, or wait and add more features first."
Rewritten as a clear question: "Should I launch my Gumroad product this Friday with what I have now, or wait 3 more weeks to add extra templates?"
Real deadline: "Friday — I already told my email list a launch is coming."
If I decide nothing: "I keep quietly delaying, and my email list loses interest."
Who else is affected: "My early subscribers who are expecting it."
Reversible or one-way: "Reversible — I can always add more later as a free update."
Whose decision is this: "Mine — nobody else needs to approve this."

A decision that isn't clearly written down always feels bigger than it really is. Once you name it clearly, most of the stress goes away — even before you've decided anything.

If you can undo it later, decide fast and fix it if you need to. If you can't undo it, slow down — but only for this one, not for every decision.
