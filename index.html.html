Name the real decision and the real deadline

Most decision fatigue isn't caused by hard decisions — it's caused by vague ones. Force the vague thing into a specific, answerable question.

A decision with a fuzzy edge always feels bigger than it is. Once it's named precisely, most of the anxiety drops — before you've decided anything.

The Ritual

The Weekly C.A.O.S. Reset

Fifteen minutes, once a week, same time if possible. This is what keeps the system alive instead of becoming a one-time exercise.

Not a to-do list. Not a journaling exercise. A filter you run your whole week through, so Monday's chaos doesn't quietly become Friday's burnout.

StageAsk Yourself
